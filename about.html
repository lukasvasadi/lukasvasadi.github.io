Interdisciplinary engineering

Before starting college, I attended a senior-level tissue engineering class at Stony Brook University and, at least for the next few years, wanted to design machines to grow human tissues for regenerative medicine.

My interests deviated after joining a small biosensor startup in the UK, where I designed electrical and mechanical systems to support a novel electrical method for quantifying drug molecule binding efficiency.

After our team raised investment capital, I returned to the US to join a tissue engineering startup, where my main focus has been building hardware and software for rapid imaging systems. Almost by accident, my early interests and later experiences came full-circle.

Along the way, I benefited from many opensource learning resources, ranging from Linux to PCB design to web development. This site is intended to contribute back to the engineering/hacker/tinkerer communities through tutorials and projects.
